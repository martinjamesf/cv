Research Study

Review of Universal Intranet Research Sessions

The User Experience Team conducted research to both inform and refine design efforts during the Compass rebuild. Here is a brief summary of studies that helped shape and influence design decisions.

Studies conducted in 2017:

  • Phase 0 (January – April)
        Analyzed results from survey (1248 responses) (1a)
        Comparative analysis with five established corporate intranets (1b)
        Empathy interviews (1c)
        Rapid Iterative Testing and Evaluation (1d)
  • Phase 1 (April – Present)
        Feedback-Driven Concept Refinement (1e)
        Feedback-Driven Acceptance Testing (1f)

Total Interviewed throughout Empathy and Feedback sessions: 84
Agent & Staff (21), Sales Leaders (15), Corp. Leaders (19), Corp. Employees (25) and Contingent (4)

Summary and action from each study:

1a. The survey was a starting point for our initial user voice. The feedback also created questions that need additional research. This, in addition to other sources, help us create a research plan.

1b. We used comparative analysis to gauge our approach and validate with users whether the features we were building were similar to other established corporate intranets. The results supported our approach and influenced plans for adding features in the future.

1c. From empathy sessions, a strong theme to personalize or customize the experience led us to run several experiments to further understand their expectations. A prototype was developed that allowed participants to customize their very own Compass homepage. Participants included various individuals across the company, including Holly Westervelt and her staff. The evidence from these experiments helped inform work on navigation and the links we’ve defaulted for users..

1d. Customization was a feature request we wanted to explore further. We created an app that allowed participants to customize their very own Compass homepage with whatever they wanted. This experiment helped inform work on navigation and the links we’ve defaulted for users.

1e. Moving from empathy, additional experiments were run to challenge various solution assumptions around learnability, ease of use, and overall satisfaction of the developing product.

1f. After the team felt more certain the solution would meet the needs of employees, agents and agent staff, a final study was done to verify the end users could use the new product to support their daily needs. The study showed participants completed 90% of the tasks with ease and the remaining 10% with some instruction and after learning the sites pattern.

Executing with an agile mindset, we continue to make adjustments based on user feedback. We shared our findings with internal and external groups that help influence our analysis. We feel our findings and recommendations are based on sound user feedback.
